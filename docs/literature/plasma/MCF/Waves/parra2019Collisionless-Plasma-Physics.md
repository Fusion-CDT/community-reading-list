This is a set of lecture notes from the first half of Oxford's MMathPhys course _Collisionless Plasma Physics_. It focuses on wave phenomena in plasmas.

Lecture note 1: Cold Plasma Waves
https://www-thphys.physics.ox.ac.uk/people/FelixParra/CollisionlessPlasmaPhysics/notes/lecV_coldplasma.pdf
[ClPP Lecture Notes 1.pdf](https://github.com/user-attachments/files/26439914/ClPP.Lecture.Notes.1.pdf)
Focuses on cold plasma waves. Felix starts with Maxwell equations, derives the general cold plasma dispersion relation, and then examines several important cases (Alfven waves, ordinary and extraordinary modes, circularly polarised waves, etc).

Lecture note 2: Wave propagation in an inhomogeneous plasma
plasma
[ClPP Lecture Notes 2.pdf](https://github.com/user-attachments/files/26439913/ClPP.Lecture.Notes.2.pdf)
Focuses on wave propagation in an inhomogeneous plasma, introduces techniques like WKB approximation and ray tracing, and discusses cutoffs and resonances.


Lecture note 3: Hot Plasma Waves
https://www-thphys.physics.ox.ac.uk/people/FelixParra/CollisionlessPlasmaPhysics/notes/lecVII_hotplasma.pdf
[ClPP Lecture Notes 3.pdf](https://github.com/user-attachments/files/26439915/ClPP.Lecture.Notes.3.pdf)
Has a light, utilitarian touch on the kinetic theory formalism that is required to describe hot plasma waves. Touches on electron cyclotron damping and electron Bernstein waves.
