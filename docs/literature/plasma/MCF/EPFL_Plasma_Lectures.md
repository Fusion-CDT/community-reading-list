This online lecture course serves as an introduction to plasma physics, starting from the kinetic description of a plasma and working through to the fluid description (MHD). The second part of this course, _Plasma Physics: Applications_, covers astrophysical plasmas, low temperature plasmas, and fusion plasmas relevant to both MCF and ICF contexts.

This is a complete lecture course including online lectures, problems with example solutions, and lecture notes.

This course was initially recommended to me by Dr Marco Cecconello as preparation of the Fusion CDT, and I recommended it to any student who prefers a visual lecture over written notes alone. If you prefer the latter, [Oxford's MMathPhys course on Collisional Plasma Physics](parra2019Collisional-Plasma-Physics-2025.md) is likely a better fit. These notes are not quite as extensive as the Oxford notes on the theory side, not covering any drift kinetics, however they go into far more practical detail in the second part of the course.

Whilst not all parts of the applications section of this course will be relevant to all students, I would still highly recommend the sections on astrophysical plasmas as it is one of the best showcases for the scientific method that I have seen.
